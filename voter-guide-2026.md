# 2026 California Primary Voter Guide — June 2, 2026

**Originally built for an Establishment Liberal voter in ZIP 92126 (Mira Mesa, San Diego); designed to be useful across the political spectrum.**

Last updated: May 10, 2026. Endorsements and polling can shift before Election Day. Verify against your official sample ballot at [sdvote.com](https://www.sdvote.com).

---

## How To Use This Guide

This guide covers every race on a 92126 (Mira Mesa) ballot for the June 2, 2026 California primary. For each race it provides:

- **Background** on each top-six candidate (officeholders get bills/votes; non-officeholders get activism/donations)
- **Topical scorecard**: positions on Housing, Climate, Public Safety, Abortion, Immigration, Education, Gaza/Israel, and Trump-era federal pushback (✓ supports / ✗ opposes / ~ mixed / ? unclear)
- **Money flags**: top donor sectors, total raised, super-PAC shadows
- **Endorsements**: key union/party/advocacy/editorial-board picks
- **Red flags** if any
- **Cross-typology recommendation row** with a confidence dot:
  - **●** HIGH — clear pick given the typology's known values
  - **◐** MED — defensible but with real trade-offs
  - **○** LOW — genuinely uncertain
  - **—** No clear pick / consider skipping
- **Counter-argument** for any pick below HIGH confidence

### The 9 Pew political typologies used

### Red-flag policy

Candidates with serious red flags (election denial, conspiracy theories, well-documented scandals) are **excluded from cross-typology picks unless they're polling top-2 and realistically viable**, in which case they get a prominent callout so voters can decide knowingly. The most prominent example in this cycle is the Governor race.

### Excluded from this guide

- **Six statewide propositions** — refer to [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/California_2026_ballot_propositions) or the [official voter guide](https://voterguide.sos.ca.gov/)

- **SD Unified School Board** (Sub-Districts B & C are up; both candidates unopposed; 92126 is in Sub-District E, not on this ballot)

# Statewide Partisan Races (Top-Two Open Primary)

California uses a top-two primary. The two candidates with the most votes — regardless of party — advance to November. Vote your conscience; strategic "lockout" votes are rarely worth it ([CalMatters analysis](https://calmatters.org/california-voter-guide-2026/governor/)).

## Governor

**The most important race on the ballot.** Newsom is termed out. Polling shows Republicans Steve Hilton (~16%) and Chad Bianco (~14%) leading a fragmented Democratic field. The California Democratic Party warned of (but doesn't expect) a possible top-two GOP lockout. The CA Republican Party failed to officially endorse a candidate (couldn't reach 60% at their April convention) ([SD Union-Tribune](https://www.sandiegouniontribune.com/2026/04/12/no-endorsement-from-california-gop-in-race-for-governor/)).

Top 6 by viability: Steve Hilton (R), Chad Bianco (R), Xavier Becerra (D), Katie Porter (D), Antonio Villaraigosa (D), Tom Steyer (D). Tony Thurmond (D), Matt Mahan (D) and others remain in the field but poll under 5%; Eric Swalwell suspended after sexual misconduct allegations; Betty Yee withdrew. ([Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/California_gubernatorial_election,_2026_(June_2_top-two_primary)))

### Xavier Becerra (D) — Former U.S. Health & Human Services Secretary, Former CA Attorney General

Becerra is the legacy Democratic establishment pick: 24 years in the U.S. House (1993-2017), CA Attorney General 2017-2021 where he filed **122 lawsuits against the first Trump administration**, then HHS Secretary under Biden. His campaign emphasizes affordability, Medi-Cal expansion, and "moving toward universal single-payer." He has a long, conventional Democratic record but no flashy signature legislation as governor candidate.

| --- | --- |
| Housing | ✓ Supply expansion, anti-price-gouging |
| Climate | ✓ Standard Dem; pro clean-energy, pro-EV |
| Public Safety | ~ Establishment Dem; pro-prosecution background |
| Abortion | ✓ Strong supporter (HHS protected access) |
| Immigration | ✓ Strongly pro-immigrant; sued Trump over family separation |
| Education | ✓ Pro public-school funding |
| Gaza/Israel | ? Has not made it a campaign issue; conventional Dem position |
| Trump pushback | ✓ Career credentials suing Trump 122 times |

**Money:** Approximately $5M+ raised, broad union and Latino-elected-official base.

**Endorsements:** SEIU California (dual with Steyer), UFCW Western States Council, California Faculty Association (dual with Thurmond), CA State Council of Laborers, ILWU, multiple Latino caucuses. ([Becerra endorsements page](https://www.xavierbecerra2026.com/endorsements/))

**Red flags:** None significant. Critics call him cautious and uninspiring.

Porter is the most prominent national-brand Democrat in the race. Three-term U.S. House member known for whiteboard-takedowns of bank executives, Big Pharma, and oil companies during committee hearings. Consumer protection law professor (UC Irvine) and former bankruptcy attorney. Lost a 2024 U.S. Senate primary, declined the Schiff-vacated seat, and pivoted to governor. Her brand is "fight Big Money, fight Big Oil."

## Lieutenant Governor

A largely ceremonial office (succession plus UC/CSU board seats), but the seat is a stepping-stone — Newsom and Kounalakis both used it. Open seat (Kounalakis termed out). Top 6 by viability: Fiona Ma (D, treasurer), Josh Fryday (D, Newsom-endorsed), Michael Tubbs (D, former Stockton mayor), Janelle Kellman (D, former Sausalito mayor), Gloria Romero (R, ex-Dem state senator), Brian Jones (R, termed-out SD state senator).

### Fiona Ma (D) — California State Treasurer (2019-present)

CPA. Three-term Treasurer, longtime Bay Area Dem. **Largest financial advantage** (~$4.6M cash on hand) and broadest endorsement coalition.

**Endorsements:** SoS Weber, Controller Cohen, Lt. Gov. Kounalakis, ~75% of the state legislature including a majority of Assembly Democrats, 50+ labor unions including CA Labor Federation, Building Trades, CPF, ILWU, Teamsters; Equality California; CA Farm Bureau. ([Fiona Ma endorsements](http://www.fionama.com/endorsements))

**Topical scorecard:** Housing ✓ supply, Climate ✓ standard, Safety ~ moderate, Abortion ✓, Immigration ✓, Education ✓, Gaza/Israel ?, Trump pushback ✓.

**Red flags:** Past sexual harassment lawsuit settled in 2022 ($55K with no admission); paid-back-tax issue 2007.

### Josh Fryday (D) — California Chief Service Officer

Newsom appointee. Former Navy JAG, former Mayor of Novato. Heads California Volunteers (largest service corps in the country). **Endorsed by Newsom** — biggest single endorsement in the race.

**Endorsements:** Gov. Newsom, CTA, former Sec. Pete Buttigieg, former Sen. Barbara Boxer.

**Topical scorecard:** Housing ✓ build 1M on public lots, Climate ✓ accelerate clean-energy permitting, Safety ~ standard, Abortion ✓, Immigration ✓, Education ✓ raise teacher pay, Gaza/Israel ?, Trump pushback ✓.

**Red flags:** No prior statewide office; some perception of Newsom-handpicking.

### Michael Tubbs (D) — Former Mayor of Stockton

National Dem star at 26 when elected mayor of Stockton (2017-21). Lost re-election in 2020. Best known for piloting a guaranteed-income program. Strong progressive brand.

**Endorsements:** **SEIU California (sole endorsement)**, Mayor Karen Bass, Mayor Barbara Lee, Dolores Huerta, Reps. Robert Garcia, Sam Liccardo, Lateefah Simon, former Sen. Laphonza Butler. ([Tubbs endorsements](https://michaeltubbsforca.com/endorsements/))

**Red flags:** Lost his Stockton re-election to a Republican; a series of unflattering local-blog posts (originally amplified by an anonymous network later linked to political operatives) hurt him in 2020.

### Janelle Kellman (D) — Former Mayor of Sausalito

Environmental attorney. Marin-County base.

**Endorsements:** Multiple Marin County Supervisors and city officials, ret. Asm. Jackie Goldberg.

**Topical scorecard:** Housing ✓, Climate ✓✓ environmental-attorney background, Safety ~, Abortion ✓, Immigration ✓, Education ✓, Gaza/Israel ?, Trump pushback ✓.

State Senator 2001-2008 (as a Democrat); switched parties in 2024. Ed reform/charter-school advocate.

San Diego County's Senate Republican leader. Termed-out from SD-40.

## Secretary of State

Manages elections and corporate filings. Incumbent **Shirley Weber (D)** is the first Black SoS in CA history. Implemented universal vote-by-mail. Three challengers, only one (Wagner) competitive.

Lara, SoP Thurmond, CA Democratic Party, CA Federation of Teachers, AFSCME, SEIU, CWA, CA Nurses Association, Planned Parenthood Affiliates of CA, Mayor Todd Gloria, SD Supervisor Monica Montgomery Steppe. ([Weber endorsements](https://drshirleyweber.com/endorsements/))

**Topical scorecard:** Housing ✓, Climate ✓, Safety ~, Abortion ✓✓, Immigration ✓, Education ✓✓, Gaza/Israel ?, Trump pushback ✓.

**Red flags:** None significant.

Orange County Supervisor, former Irvine mayor, ex-Asm. Voter-ID/election-integrity platform. Endorsed by CA Republican Party, Reform California, CRA.

**Topical scorecard:** Voter ID ✓✓, Vote-by-mail rollback ✓, etc. Anti-existing-election-systems framing.

## Controller

# Ballot Measure

## Measure A — Non-Primary Homes (Empty Homes) Tax

**The Question:** Should San Diego tax homes that are not the owner's primary residence and are vacant for 183+ days per year? ([KPBS explainer](https://www.kpbs.org/news/politics/2026/04/20/2026-primary-election-measure-a-non-primary-homes-tax-empty-homes))

**Mechanism:**

- **$8,000/year** in 2027, **$10,000/year** in 2028+
- **+$4,000 corporate-owner surcharge** in 2027, **+$5,000** in 2028+
- Inflation-indexed annually after 2028
- **Exempts** primary residences, properties rented to tenants, properties used by family members, and hardship cases (military service, disaster damage, probate, long-term care, death)
- Revenue goes to the city general fund (not earmarked)
- Estimated **$9.2-21.4M** year 1, **$10.5-24.3M** year 2

**Arguments for:**

- Encourages converting vacant homes into available rentals (housing-supply lever)
- Generates revenue from non-resident property owners during a housing crisis
- Falls primarily on wealthier owners holding empty second homes
- Council passed it 8-1, signaling broad local political consensus

**Arguments against:**

- General fund deposit means no spending guarantee
- Adds to already-high tax burden
- Property-rights and enforcement concerns (how does the city verify occupancy?)
- Risk of hitting "house-rich/cash-poor" inheritors and elderly absentee owners harder than wealthy speculators

### Cross-typology recommendation — Measure A

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| PL | **Yes** | ● | Pro-supply + redistributive tax on wealth |
| EL | **Yes** | ◐ | Aligns with modest-bigger-government values; concern about general-fund earmark |

| PR | **No** | ● | Standard anti-tax populist position |

| FF | **No** | ◐ | Same |

**Counter-arguments:**

- **EL** (Yes ◐): But consider **No** if the lack of revenue earmark concerns you — voters could push for an earmarked version next cycle.
- **AR/FF** (No ◐): But consider **Yes** if you accept that this is a behavioral lever (encouraging rentals) more than a revenue measure.
